# Sync Camera Clip Space And Depth Math

This PR aligns the camera contract between `voxelized-js`, `three-voxel`, and the demo shader so that frustum culling, camera bridging, and logarithmic depth all interpret clip space the same way. The voxel camera now exposes its projection parameters and accepts a sync callback, which lets `three-voxel` either copy a Three.js camera into voxel state or rebuild a Three.js camera from voxel state without leaving the two views half a frame apart. In parallel, culling now distinguishes WebGPU and WebGL clip-space conventions, and the demo shader uses the same near and far planes as the camera when mapping logarithmic depth. This addresses #27.

The quantitative reason for the change is straightforward. The engine keeps 16 visible regions live, with 4 more queued for prebuild and 4 for prefetch. A single culling false negative therefore removes about 6.25% of the active render budget. Because each region spans 256 by 256 world units, one mistaken edge rejection drops roughly 65 thousand square units of terrain from the visible set. If camera drift or clip-space mismatch flips even two or three boundary regions, the visible hole grows to roughly 130 thousand to 200 thousand square units. By making FOV, near plane, eye height, and clip convention consistent across both camera paths, this PR moves those errors back toward the exact frustum boundary instead of letting them appear during ordinary camera motion.

The depth correction has a similar scale. With a near plane of 0.1 and a far plane of 4000, the frustum spans a 40,000 to 1 ratio, which is about 15.3 steps in log2 space. The previous shader normalization baked in a different range and added an implicit offset before the logarithm, so a small mismatch near the camera was being amplified across the whole depth interval. This PR derives the logarithmic factor directly from `far / near` and normalizes clip `w` against `near`, which should materially reduce long-range z fighting and horizon shimmer while keeping the depth curve tied to the actual camera parameters.

I validated the package builds for `voxelized-js` and `three-voxel` after these changes. The demo production build still fails under `vite-plugin-top-level-await` because the current target configuration rejects transformed destructuring, so the practical review surface here is the camera bridge, the clip-aware frustum culling, and the logarithmic depth fix.
